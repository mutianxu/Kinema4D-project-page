Kinema4D:
Kinematic-Controlled 4D World Modeling for Spatial-Aware Robotic Simulation

Mutian Xu1, Tianbao Zhang2,
Tianqi Liu1, Zhaoxi Chen1, Xiaoguang Han2, Ziwei Liu1†
1S-Lab, Nanyang Technological University    2SSE, CUHKSZ   
†Corresponding Author

We introduce Kinema4D, the first 4D generative robotic simulator.

Abstract

While a few recent generative models show promise in simulating robot-environment interactions, they primarily operate in 2D pixel space, failing to capture the explicit 3D spatial constraints where the interaction is inherently defined. To this end, we propose Kinema4D, a framework that generates future robot-world interactions grounded in 4D space. At the input level, given a single-view RGB image and an action sequence, we drive a 3D-reconstructed, URDF-based robot model via kinematics to produce a 4D robot trajectory, which is then projected into a pointmap sequence. Through this programmatic process, we not only decouple robot motion from environment synthesis but also represent robotic actions as a deterministic spatial-aware visual control signal. Accordingly, at the output level, the generative model purely synthesizes the environment's response to this control guidance while simultaneously predicting synchronized RGB and pointmap sequences, enabling the model to perceive and manipulate the world with full spatial awareness. To train our framework, we curated a large-scale dataset of real-world and synthetic demonstrations with high-quality 4D annotations. Extensive experiments show that Kinema4D ensures absolute kinematic correctness while generalizing to simulate diverse real-world dynamics.


Method Overview

An overview of our framework. 1) Kinematics Control: Given a 3D robot with its URDF, and an action sequence, we drive the 3D robot via kinematics to produce a 4D robot trajectory, which is then projected into a pointmap sequence. This process re-represents raw actions as a spatial visual signal. 2) 4D Generative World Modeling: This signal and the world image are sent to a shared VAE encoder and then fused with an occupancy-aligned robot mask and Gaussian noise, which are finally denoised by a Diffusion Transformer to generate a full future 4D (pointmap+RGB) world sequence.
